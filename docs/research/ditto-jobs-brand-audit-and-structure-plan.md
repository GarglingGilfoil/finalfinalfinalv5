# 1. Executive Summary

Ditto.Work is not generic SaaS marketing. In practice it behaves like recruitment operations infrastructure with premium restraint. The live site combines a calm but assertive editorial voice, a tightly controlled blue-gray surface system, rounded glass-like UI shells, direct utility-led messaging, and motion that feels technical rather than playful. Its strongest brand trait is not novelty. It is operational confidence.

Ditto Jobs should inherit that confidence almost exactly at the system level. The typography behavior, color hierarchy, surface treatment, button/nav/footer DNA, legal shell quality, motion restraint, and overall polish bar should carry forward with very little deviation. That is what keeps the rebuild visibly inside the same family.

Ditto Jobs must still become its own product type. A public job board is candidate-facing, search-heavy, index-sensitive, and content-variable. That means the home page must prioritize search and discovery above narrative storytelling, results pages must be clearer and more SEO-disciplined than a generic marketing site, and job detail pages must put comprehension and conversion ahead of visual flourish. The correct move is not to clone Ditto.Work page-for-page. The correct move is to transplant its brand system into a stronger public information architecture built for search, browse, compare, and apply.

# 2. Ditto.Work Audit

## System-Level Findings

### Brand Character
- The brand feels product-led, operational, and composed.
- It sits closer to software infrastructure than friendly SaaS.
- The emotional register is calm authority rather than warmth, hype, or play.
- The site is premium because it is disciplined, not because it is luxurious.
- Distinctiveness comes from turning ATS and recruitment complexity into something visually ordered and controlled.

### Typography System
- The live site uses `DM Sans`.
- Headings are large, tight, and assertive, often with slightly compressed tracking.
- Body copy is controlled and readable, with moderate density tolerated when the information is useful.
- Eyebrows and category labels often use uppercase or high-contrast small-label behavior.
- Sentence-case messaging dominates. It reads like a product operator wrote it, not an ad copy team.
- Typography creates authority by being direct and unornamented.

### Color System
- The site runs on pale blue-gray backgrounds, white or near-white surfaces, dark ink text, and a bright structural blue accent.
- Observed core tokens from the shipped CSS include `#d6d8de`, `#e8edf3`, `#f7f9fc`, `#ffffff`, `#0f141d`, `#4d596b`, `#18b5ff`, `#0f8ecf`, `#085e8b`, and `#04354f`.
- Saturated blue is used as a structural accent, CTA driver, highlight, and motion energy source.
- Gradients are present, but they are controlled and integrated into the surface system rather than used as decorative wallpaper.
- Contrast philosophy is pragmatic: soft surfaces with strong ink, then concentrated accent energy where attention matters.

### Layout System
- The layout feels grid-disciplined and max-width controlled.
- Sections tend to be framed as deliberate bands or shells rather than loose page sprawl.
- There is a strong preference for balanced left-right compositions and contained cards/panels.
- Hero sections do not overfill the screen with illustration. They preserve text authority.
- Section spacing is generous but not airy to the point of feeling editorial or luxury-first.
- The system supports both broad marketing bands and denser product/detail panels.

### UI System
- Buttons are rounded, pill-like, and consistently hierarchical.
- Nav and footer DNA are strong enough to be reused directly.
- Panels and cards rely on subtle borders, soft shadows, controlled radii, and layered surfaces.
- Pricing, FAQ, forms, and legal pages all show the same system discipline even when the content model changes.
- The design system route confirms the brand already supports tables, chips, filters, overlays, command surfaces, and dense utility patterns.

### Motion and Art Direction
- Motion is technical, polished, and restrained.
- The brand uses gradient glows, light traces, diagrams, floating system panels, and animated route lines.
- Motion adds a sense of active software systems rather than entertainment.
- This approach works on marketing and product storytelling surfaces, but would become noise if overused on results and detail pages.

### Content and Conversion Structure
- The site explains value through clear sequences: promise, proof, capability, credibility, conversion.
- Copy is declarative and specific. It does not hide behind startup cliches.
- Microcopy tends to be short and functional.
- The brand tolerates fairly dense information when the structure is strong.
- Conversion paths are direct. The pricing and contact pages are especially practical.

### Consistency and Exceptions
- The global system truth is visible across home, product, pricing, FAQ, contact, privacy, and terms.
- The About page is more editorial and manifesto-like, but still brand-consistent.
- The legal shell is unusually polished and should be treated as canonical.
- The design-system route is not a throwaway internal page. It reveals the brand's true utility depth and should heavily influence Ditto Jobs.

## Page-by-Page Findings

### Home
- The home page is structured around “Recruitment. Running at scale.”
- It emphasizes system thinking, operating rhythm, and proof of breadth.
- The hero balances assertive copy with branded system visuals.
- The page is not search-centric, which is correct for Ditto.Work but not sufficient for Ditto Jobs.

### Product
- The product page is the clearest articulation of the brand's operating-system mindset.
- Its Source / Engage / Place framing shows how Ditto narrates workflow without becoming cluttered.
- Sticky progress behavior and modular feature sections demonstrate a reusable section logic for future public content.

### Pricing
- Pricing is practical, transparent, and calculator-led.
- This page is a strong reference for utilitarian information density, interaction framing, and conversion sequencing.
- It proves the brand can handle structured tools, numeric UI, and next-step explanation cleanly.

### FAQ
- FAQ uses categorized density well.
- The page demonstrates how Ditto handles large content volumes without losing clarity.
- It is a useful reference for future help, support, or public policy content for Ditto Jobs.

### About
- About is more expressive and narrative-driven.
- It reveals the company's self-image: builders fixing broken systems with high standards.
- This informs tone and confidence, but its layout should not be over-transferred onto core job-board utility pages.

### Contact
- Contact combines brand framing with a direct form.
- It is useful precedent for future candidate-contact or employer-interest surfaces because the tone remains practical.

### Privacy and Terms
- These pages use a premium legal shell with sticky section navigation and mobile-adapted structure.
- That shell should be carried forward almost exactly for Ditto Jobs legal and policy routes.
- These pages also confirm Ditto Jobs already exists inside the company's legal domain model.

### Sign-In and Auth Pattern
- The sign-in shell uses a split layout with a branded side and a utilitarian form side.
- The structure is transferable, but the copy and friction model would need to change for candidate-facing flows.

### Design System
- This route is one of the most important references for the rebuild.
- It proves the sister brand already knows how to present tables, filters, empty states, mobile adaptations, overlays, file workflows, and operational UI.
- The strongest implication is that Ditto Jobs should not invent a separate visual language for utility screens.

## Asset Conclusions

- The canonical digital brand lockups are the standard `black on white` and `white on black` variants.
- The blue-only marks are secondary accents, not the default brand signature.
- The `-1` variants appear to behave like fallback or mono exports and should be treated cautiously until a human confirms intended use.
- The symbol mark is best for compact interfaces, loading states, and small branded components.
- The wordmark should own primary brand placements such as the site header and footer.
- The upload does not justify inventing a broader illustration system. That should come from the live-site language instead.

# 4. Sister Brand Transfer Model

## Carry Over Directly
- `DM Sans` as the core typeface.
- The blue-gray, white, ink, and bright-blue color hierarchy.
- Rounded control philosophy and radius behavior.
- Soft glass-like surface treatment with disciplined borders and shadows.
- Header and footer structural DNA.
- CTA hierarchy and button behavior.
- Legal shell quality and structured navigation behavior.
- Motion restraint, reduced-motion support, and technical rather than playful animation language.
- The direct, product-operator voice.
- The overall polish threshold. Ditto Jobs should feel finished, not experimental.

## Adapt for Job Board Context
- Home page structure must become search-first instead of narrative-first.
- Search and location entry should sit above the fold on home and results pages.
- Filter chips, panels, and mobile filter drawers should use Ditto surface language but be optimized for repeated scanning.
- Job cards need to translate the card system into a clearer information hierarchy with title, company, location, work mode, employment type, and salary logic.
- Job detail should reuse brand shells and typography while prioritizing scannability and sticky conversion.
- Category and location landing pages should feel like real public discovery pages, not thin marketing stubs.
- Empty states and loading states should use the symbol mark and soft motion sparingly.
- Candidate-facing copy must stay direct but become more supportive and less enterprise-operator-centric.

## Reject or Avoid
- Overly abstract marketing sections before the user can search for jobs.
- Excessive diagrammatic hero art on results or detail pages.
- Decorative motion on dense utility screens.
- Enterprise-style software density where candidate comprehension should win.
- Copy that sounds like an ATS platform when the user is a job seeker.
- Overbuilding company-profile surfaces before the search/detail fundamentals are strong.
- Indexing every filter combination and creating crawl sprawl.

# 5. Ditto Jobs Information Architecture

- `/jobs/:jobSlug`
- `/jobs/category/:categorySlug`
- `/jobs/location/:locationSlug`
- `/about`
- `/contact`
- `/privacy`
- `/terms`

## Recommended Conditional Routes

- `/companies/:companySlug`
- `/jobs/featured`
- `/jobs/remote`
- `/help`

These should be added only if content depth supports them. A weak company profile surface is worse than a strong company summary module inside job cards and detail pages.

## Page Hierarchy Logic

### Home
- Search-led entry point
- Brand framing
- Curated exploration
- Featured jobs and categories
- Trust and credibility
- Footer navigation into deeper browsing

### Jobs Index
- Canonical browse/search route
- Supports query, filter, sort, and pagination
- Can also act as the canonical target for many non-indexed filtered states

### Job Detail
- Primary conversion page
- Strong metadata block above the fold
- Structured long-form content below
- Related discovery after core comprehension

### Category and Location Pages
- Public SEO landing pages with useful intros, job lists, and internal links
- These should not be thin doorway pages

### Company Strategy
- Start with company summaries inside cards and details
- Add standalone company pages only when there is enough employer content, branding, and active listings to justify them

## Front-End-Friendly Structural Model

- Shared `PublicShell`
- `HomePage`
- `JobsIndexPage`
- `CategoryLandingPage`
- `LocationLandingPage`
- `JobDetailPage`
- `LegalPageShell`
- Shared sections for `HeroSearch`, `FeaturedJobs`, `FeaturedCategories`, `TrustBar`, `JobList`, `FilterPanel`, `Pagination`, `CompanySummary`, and `RelatedJobs`

# 6. Homepage Structure Recommendation

## Recommended Section Order

1. Global header
2. Hero with value proposition and immediate search
3. Quick-pick discovery row
4. Featured jobs
5. Category exploration
6. Why Ditto Jobs is credible
7. Featured employers or employer proof
8. Candidate value section
9. Secondary exploration band
10. Final CTA and footer

## Section-by-Section Rationale

### 1. Global Header
- Use the Ditto wordmark, restrained nav, and high-clarity primary CTA.
- Keep it lighter than a SaaS product nav. This is a public discovery surface.

### 2. Hero with Search
- The hero should immediately answer what the site is for and give the user a search action.
- Preferred structure: headline, supporting sentence, search input pair, CTA, then a restrained branded visual treatment.
- The visual should support the system identity without delaying job discovery.

### 3. Quick-Pick Discovery Row
- Show high-signal entry points such as remote roles, Cape Town, Johannesburg, software engineering, operations, or other real content clusters once data is known.
- This lets the home page act as both brand surface and discovery launcher.

### 4. Featured Jobs
- Use editorial curation rather than a giant feed.
- Show a small set of strong cards with meaningful metadata and clear paths into search.

### 5. Category Exploration
- Present categories as structured landing-path entry points.
- This is important for both browsing behavior and public SEO architecture.

### 6. Why Ditto Jobs Is Credible
- Use brief proof blocks, employer counts if accurate later, or a process statement about quality roles and transparent hiring.
- Do not overclaim. Credibility should feel measured.

### 7. Featured Employers or Employer Proof
- Only include if the content is real and not placeholder-heavy.
- If logos are weak early on, use concise employer-quality framing instead.

### 8. Candidate Value Section
- Explain why this board is more useful than generic listings.
- Keep the language grounded in clarity, fit, and efficiency.

### 9. Secondary Exploration Band
- This can surface location pages, popular searches, or newly added roles.
- It should support deeper browsing without crowding the first screen.

### 10. Final CTA and Footer
- Use the sister-brand footer DNA.
- Final CTA can route users back to search or to featured jobs rather than generic “get started” language.

# 7. Search, Results, and Job Detail Structure

## Search and Browse Model

### Default Search Behavior
- Default to the freshest relevant jobs.
- Search should accept free text plus location.
- If no query is entered, the page should still return a meaningful browse state rather than an empty prompt-only page.

### Filters
- Location
- Category or department
- Employment type
- Work mode: remote, hybrid, onsite
- Optional later filters: salary visibility, company, experience level

### Sorting
- `Newest`
- `Featured`
- Optional later: `Closing soon`

### Results Presentation
- Use clear vertical cards with strong scan order:
  1. job title
  2. company
  3. location
  4. work mode
  5. employment type
  6. salary when visible
  7. short summary
  8. posted date or freshness cue
- Featured jobs should use a modest badge treatment, not a loud redesign.

### Pagination vs Infinite Scroll
- Recommend paginated results, not infinite scroll.
- Pagination is better for SEO, sharable URLs, back-button recovery, and user orientation.
- Infinite scroll can be considered later only for controlled non-indexed contexts.

### Mobile Filter Approach
- Use a bottom sheet or full-screen drawer with explicit apply and clear actions.
- Keep active filters visible as chips above results.

### Zero Results State
- Explain why results are empty in direct language.
- Offer quick reset options.
- Offer nearby categories or locations.
- Optionally show featured jobs as a fallback, clearly labeled.

### Loading State
- Use skeleton cards and preserved page structure.
- Avoid full-page spinners except for route-level transitions where there is no stable shell yet.

## Job Detail Model

### Above the Fold
- Job title
- Company name and compact company summary cue
- Location
- Work mode
- Employment type
- Salary if visible
- Status cues such as posted recently or closed
- Primary apply CTA
- Optional save/share/report actions kept secondary

### Company Info
- Start with a company summary card rather than a full company profile page dependency.
- Include logo if available, but handle no-logo gracefully with a consistent fallback mark or initials block.

### Salary Treatment
- Salary should be explicit when available.
- When not available, do not fake precision. Either omit cleanly or use a truthful visibility label if the data model supports it.

### Description Handling
- Break long descriptions into sections such as overview, responsibilities, requirements, benefits, and process.
- Support both short and long jobs without collapsing layout integrity.
- Preserve line length, spacing, and heading structure for readability.

### Sticky Apply Logic
- On desktop, use a sticky side card if the viewport allows.
- On mobile, use a persistent bottom CTA or strong inline apply pattern near the top and after key sections.
- Closed roles should replace the apply CTA with an explicit unavailable state and adjacent discovery options.

### Related Jobs
- Show related jobs after the main description and company context.
- Relatedness should prefer category, location, and company adjacency when available.
- Support empty related states without creating dead space.

### Trust Signals
- Posted date freshness
- Application method clarity
- Employer identity clarity
- Structured job metadata
- Transparent salary handling when present

## Apply Journey Assumptions

- The public surface should assume low friction.
- Users should not be forced into auth before understanding the role.
- The system should support both direct apply and continue/apply-later patterns as future expansion.
- Public pages should preview what happens next without exposing unnecessary flow complexity.

# 8. Design System Foundation

## Tokens and Primitives

### Typography Roles
- Display headline
- Hero support copy
- Section title
- Card title
- Body
- Small metadata
- Eyebrow or label

### Color Roles
- App background
- Soft background
- Surface
- Strong surface
- Primary ink
- Secondary ink
- Brand blue primary
- Brand blue secondary
- Deep accent blue
- Border and divider
- Success, warning, error states added later without breaking the core palette

### Spacing and Radius
- Use a compact but breathable spacing scale.
- Keep radii aligned with the live system's rounded control philosophy.
- Observed radii of `12`, `18`, and `24` are strong starting anchors.

### Borders, Shadows, and Glow
- Thin borders
- Soft layered shadows
- Controlled gradient glows only where they help emphasis
- No heavy neumorphism or dramatic floating-card theatrics

### Layout Widths
- One primary content width for major public pages
- One denser content width for legal and structured text pages
- One wider utility layout for results pages where filters and lists coexist

### Motion Rules
- Calm transitions
- Soft reveals
- Technical highlight motion
- Respect reduced motion
- Avoid decorative movement in results/detail flows

## Components to Design First

1. Global header and footer
2. Hero search module
3. Search input pair and submit action
4. Primary and secondary button system
5. Job card
6. Filter chip
7. Filter drawer or filter sidebar
8. Sort control
9. Company mark block with no-logo fallback
10. Sticky apply card
11. Metadata row pattern
12. Long-form content section shell
13. Empty state
14. Loading skeleton
15. Pagination controls

## Page Templates to Design First

# 9. Voice and Content Rules

## What Transfers from Ditto.Work
- Direct, declarative sentences
- Product confidence without fluff
- Tight microcopy
- Clear headings that say what the section is actually for
- Limited marketing exaggeration

## What Must Change for Candidate-Facing Surfaces
- The tone should stay confident but become more supportive.
- Job seekers need clarity, reassurance, and momentum.
- The language must not sound like back-office recruiting software.
- Utility labels should be plainer than the sister product's platform messaging.

## Content Rules for Ditto Jobs

- Prefer concrete nouns over abstract positioning language.
- Say what the user can do next.
- Keep metadata labels short and familiar.
- Avoid inflated promises about dream jobs, perfect matches, or career transformation.
- Do not hide missing information with polished euphemisms.
- If salary is unavailable, say less rather than inventing soft filler.
- If a role is closed, say it clearly and redirect usefully.
- Category and location pages should read like helpful discovery pages, not SEO-stuffed landing pages.

## Copy Register by Surface

### Home
- Clear, inviting, search-led, lightly branded

### Results
- Efficient, comparative, low-friction

### Job Detail
- Informative, structured, trustworthy

### Apply Entry
- Reassuring, concise, expectation-setting

### Legal and Support
- Plain, careful, and highly scannable

# 10. SEO and Public Architecture Recommendations

## Indexing Strategy

- Index the home page.
- Index `/jobs`.
- Index job detail pages.
- Index high-quality category landing pages.
- Index high-quality location landing pages.
- Do not index arbitrary filter combinations.
- Keep auth, saved-state, and apply-continuation routes non-indexed.

## Canonical Strategy

- Use canonical URLs on paginated results and landing pages.
- Preserve sharable search state in the URL.
- Ensure non-indexed filter combinations canonicalize back to the most appropriate stable route when needed.
- Avoid multiple public URLs representing the same job detail.

## Schema Recommendations

- `WebSite` with search action on the public shell
- `ItemList` for browse and landing pages
- `JobPosting` for job detail pages
- `BreadcrumbList` for structured public hierarchies
- `Organization` where employer or Ditto entity context is appropriate

## Internal Linking Model

- Home should link to featured jobs, categories, and locations.
- Results should link cleanly to detail pages with descriptive anchors.
- Job details should link to related jobs plus relevant category and location pages.
- Category and location pages should cross-link where helpful without creating spammy grids.

## Metadata Rules

- Titles should prioritize role, location, and brand.
- Descriptions should remain factual and readable.
- Category and location pages need unique metadata, not token-swapped templates.
- Social cards should use the canonical wordmark and restrained brand surfaces.

# 11. Risks and Failure Modes

## Branding Risks
- Copying Ditto.Work's visuals without adapting its product logic will produce a polished but ineffective job board.
- Using blue-only or fallback logo variants as the primary lockup could quietly degrade brand consistency.
- Overdecorating results and detail pages would dilute the operational confidence that makes the sister brand distinctive.

## IA Risks
- Overbuilding company pages too early could fragment focus and content quality.
- Weak category or location pages would create thin surfaces that hurt both user value and SEO quality.

## SEO Risks
- Indexing every filter state would create crawl sprawl and duplicate-intent pages.
- Thin landing pages would weaken the public architecture.
- Infinite-scroll-first thinking would complicate shareability and recovery.

## Responsive Risks
- Search and filter interactions can easily become clumsy on mobile if the filter model is too broad.
- Sticky apply behavior can become obstructive or visually heavy if not adapted per viewport.

## Content Risks
- Job data will be inconsistent. The design must handle missing logos, hidden salaries, short descriptions, long descriptions, and closed roles.
- Generic candidate-marketing copy would push the rebuild toward commodity job-board territory.

## Over-Design Risks
- Trying to make every screen a hero moment will reduce usefulness.
- Excessive animation or gradients would make high-frequency browsing feel tiring.

## Under-Design Risks
- Treating the board as pure utility with no brand texture would break sister-brand continuity.
- Settling for generic templates would waste the strongest advantage this rebuild already has: a distinctive live brand system.

# 12. Recommended Next Design Sequence

1. Lock the public shell foundation.
   - Header, footer, typography roles, color tokens, surface rules, button system.
2. Design the hero-search module.
   - This determines home, search entry, and global search behavior.
3. Design the job card and metadata row.
   - This is the core repeated object across home, results, related jobs, and landing pages.
4. Design the results-page filter and pagination system.
   - This sets the utility architecture and mobile interaction model.
5. Design the job detail headline and sticky apply pattern.
   - This resolves the highest-stakes conversion surface.
6. Design the long-form description shell and company summary block.
   - This validates scannability under content variability.
7. Design category and location landing templates.
   - This secures public discovery architecture before SEO implementation.
8. Design empty, loading, closed-role, and no-logo states.
   - These states should be solved before build, not patched later.
9. Design legal and info shells.
   - These can borrow heavily from Ditto.Work and close quickly.
10. Design future auth and apply-entry surfaces only after the public discovery model is stable.

## What Must Be Validated Before Code Starts

- Final route architecture
- Search parameter model
- Canonical logo usage
- Core job card metadata priority
- Mobile filter interaction
- Closed-role behavior
- Salary visibility logic
